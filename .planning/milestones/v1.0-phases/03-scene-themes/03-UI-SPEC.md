> Visual and interaction contract for the four full-screen running scenes (Shrinking Disc,
> Night to Sunrise, Walking Home, Car on a Road) that replace
> `lib/screens/placeholder_running_screen.dart`. Generated by gsd-ui-researcher, verified by
> gsd-ui-checker.
>
> **Fidelity note:** Per this project's `CLAUDE.md` and `03-CONTEXT.md` D-01, all four scenes'
> colors, geometry, thresholds, and motion formulas are **final**, transcribed directly from
> `design/README.md` ¬ß¬ßC‚ÄìF and cross-referenced against the working prototype
> `design/Zual.dc.html` for exact pixel/percentage values `design/README.md` states only
> qualitatively (sun glow growth, hill silhouette geometry, cloud placement, decorative-loop
> timings). Where the two sources disagree or `design/README.md` is vague, `Zual.dc.html` is
> the more authoritative source (same precedent set by `02-UI-SPEC.md`'s scene mini-previews).
> Where this contract's defaults (8pt spacing scale, "3‚Äì4 type sizes / 2 weights", 60/30/10
> color split) would normally apply, they are explicitly overridden below ‚Äî this phase renders
> zero text and its full-bleed illustrative palette does not map to a UI-chrome color split.
>
> **Important correction to `03-CONTEXT.md`/`03-RESEARCH.md` D-02:** those documents state the
> design doc specifies decorative-loop motion only qualitatively, "without exact timings," and

> **final, locked** values below, not the "recommended defaults" framing. This does not change
> any product decision (the recommended defaults were already extremely close), but the source
> of truth is upgraded from "discretion" to "locked spec" ‚Äî see Decorative Loop Contract below.

## Spacing Scale

**Full exception to the standard 8pt scale ‚Äî do not apply it.** Every dimension in this phase is
a literal pixel or percentage value transcribed verbatim from `design/README.md` ¬ß¬ßC‚ÄìF and
`Zual.dc.html` (locked per D-01). Values are frequently not multiples of 4 or 8 (e.g. 340px,
310px, 74px‚Üíwait, 300px, 72px, 130px, 26px, 38px, 50px, 110px, 42px, 28px). Do not round or
substitute ‚Äî see the per-scene Geometry tables below for every exact value.

**Units convention (important for implementation):**
- Values given as literal `px` are **fixed logical pixels**, identical on every screen size ‚Äî do
  **not** scale them by device width/height.
- Values given as `%` are **fractions of the full-bleed canvas** (`size.width` / `size.height`
  in the `CustomPainter`), and must be computed as `size.width * pct` / `size.height * pct` at
  paint time, not hardcoded.
- Combined values (e.g. `bottom: calc(20% + 6px)`) mean: percentage of canvas height/width, plus
  a fixed pixel offset ‚Äî compute both terms and add them.

Exceptions: entire scale is an intentional exception to the standard 8pt default, locked by
`design/README.md`'s fidelity requirement ‚Äî consistent with `02-UI-SPEC.md`'s same override for
the Setup screen.

## Scene Geometry & Motion Specs

Common conventions for all four scenes:
- Canvas fills the entire screen (`size` passed to `CustomPainter.paint` == full available
  space inside `RunningScreen`), portrait only, per project constraints.
- `progress` (`p`) is the single input every formula below reads; `remaining` (`r`) = `1 - p`
  where used. Both are always pre-clamped to `0..1` by `TimerController` before reaching a
  scene, but re-clamp defensively inside pure formula functions per `03-RESEARCH.md` Pattern 3.
- All CSS `transition:` durations from `Zual.dc.html` (e.g. `transform 0.12s linear`,
  `background 0.4s linear`) are a **web-specific smoothing artifact** and must **not** be
  reproduced in Flutter ‚Äî per `03-RESEARCH.md` Pattern 1, the per-scene `Ticker` already samples
  `progress` fresh every frame (~16ms), so motion is already continuous and correctly paced by
  wall-clock time; adding an extra CSS-style ease/duration on top would introduce lag between
  the shared progress value and what's drawn. Draw every progress-driven value directly, with no
  additional interpolation/easing layer.

### Arrival Mechanic (shared by Walking Home and Car on a Road ‚Äî SCENE-03, SCENE-04)

```
arrivalLeftFraction(p) = (6 + p.clamp(0.0, 1.0) * 62) / 100
```
At `p = 0`: character/car sits at 6% from the left edge. At `p = 1`: sits at 68% from the left
edge ‚Äî visually at the house's door, since the house group is anchored to the right side
(`right: 26px`) with its door centered within its own 112px-wide body. **Distance remaining
visually tracks time remaining exactly** ‚Äî this is the core "readable at a glance" mechanic for
both scenes and must not be adjusted, eased, or clamped differently than this formula.

## Decorative Loop Contract (locked, per corrected D-02)

All three decorative loops below are **continuous, independent of `progress`**, and are hosted
by the same per-scene `Ticker` that samples `progress` (per `03-RESEARCH.md` Pattern 1/2) ‚Äî never
a second `AnimationController`. Exact keyframe values (already present in `Zual.dc.html`, not
"discretionary defaults"):

| Loop | Applies to | Period | Curve | Motion |
|------|-----------|--------|-------|--------|
| `bob` | Walking Home character group | 0.62s, repeating | ease-in-out | `translateY`: 0 ‚Üí ‚àí7px ‚Üí 0 (vertical bob, anchored at bottom) |
| `spin` | Car on a Road, both wheels | 0.7s, repeating | linear | Continuous rotation, 0¬∞‚Üí360¬∞ |
| `twinkle` | Night to Sunrise, each of the 28 stars | 3s, repeating, **staggered per-star delay** `(i % 6) * 0.5s` | ease-in-out | Opacity oscillates `0.35 ‚Üí 1 ‚Üí 0.35` (multiplied by that star's base opacity and the overall `starOpacity(p)` fade ‚Äî three multiplicative opacity factors combine: per-star base, twinkle oscillation, and progress-driven fade) |

**Freeze-on-non-running rule (Claude's Discretion, confirmed):** the per-scene `Ticker` ‚Äî and
therefore every decorative loop above ‚Äî stops entirely whenever `TimerController.phase` is not
`running` (covers both `paused` and `done`). At the moment of stopping, the last-rendered
decorative phase (bob offset, wheel angle, twinkle opacity) freezes in place rather than
snapping to a rest position ‚Äî this produces a calm static frame rather than an abrupt animation
cut, consistent with "the active scene settles into its end state" (`design/README.md` ¬ßH). This
is Phase 4's cue to layer the chime/pill on top of an already-calm frame; this phase only needs
to guarantee the freeze behavior, not the chime/pill themselves.

## Interaction Contract

- **Progress-in, pixels-out only (SCENE-05):** every scene reads exactly one input,
  `TimerController.progress` (a `double` 0..1), via the `SceneRenderer` contract. No scene reads
  `TimerController.phase`, `.theme`, or any other controller state directly for its *painting*
  logic ‚Äî phase is only watched to start/stop the local `Ticker` (per `03-RESEARCH.md` Pattern
  1), never passed into a painter's draw formulas.
- **Nothing tappable, anywhere:** no scene may add its own `GestureDetector`, `InkWell`,
  `GestureRecognizer`, or any tap/long-press/drag handler. The (Phase 4) hidden long-press
  recognizer lives on `RunningScreen`/`SceneHost` ‚Äî the composition root ‚Äî never inside a scene
  widget. This is a hard requirement verified by the widget test
  `test/scenes/scene_renderer_test.dart` (per `03-RESEARCH.md`'s Wave 0 Gaps): no
  gesture-reactive ancestor exists anywhere in a mounted scene's subtree.
- **Smooth motion is a visual requirement, not just a performance one:** all four scenes must
  visibly move at native frame rate (per `03-RESEARCH.md`'s Pitfall 1 finding ‚Äî polling
  `TimerController.progress` only via its 200ms `notifyListeners()` cadence produces visibly
  stepped/jerky motion and fails SCENE-05 outright). This is a design-contract requirement here
  because it directly affects whether the disc's shrink, the sunrise's sky shift, and the
  character/car's walk read as calm and continuous versus mechanical and janky ‚Äî central to the
  app's "playful but calm" tone, not a nice-to-have.
- **End-of-run visual correctness (feeds Phase 4):** at `progress = 1`, each scene must already
  be correct and calm on its own, before Phase 4 adds the chime/pill:
  - Shrinking Disc: disc scaled to its `0.001` floor (visually "gone"), color at pure red
    (`#DE6A4B`, since `remaining = 0`).
  - Night to Sunrise: stars and moon fully faded (opacity 0, well before `p=1` per the fade
    formulas), sun at its highest point (`top = 22%`), hill fully warm (`#6E9060`).
  - Walking Home: character horizontally at 68% from the left, visually at the house door.
  - Car on a Road: car horizontally at 68% from the left, visually at the house, wheels frozen
    (per the freeze-on-`done` rule above).
- **No new packages, no new state layer:** confirmed by `03-RESEARCH.md` ‚Äî every technique here
  (`CustomPainter`, `Canvas`, `Ticker`, gradients, `Color.lerp`) is Flutter SDK-native; this
  UI-SPEC does not introduce any requirement that would need a new dependency.
